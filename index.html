How SoundView Works

One glance in, one clear sentence out.

01

Capture

A wide-angle camera on the frame continuously reads the space ahead — sidewalks, doorways, signage, faces, obstacles.

02

Process

An onboard microcontroller runs a lightweight AI model that identifies what matters most in the current scene, in real time.

03

Describe

The scene is turned into a short, plain-language description — "curb ahead," "door on your left," "person approaching."

04

Feedback

That description is spoken through bone-conduction audio, so ears stay free to hear the real world at the same time.

Questions

Frequently asked

What is Project Axis?

Project Axis is a student-led initiative building affordable, AI-powered smart glasses for people with vision impairment. We design, prototype, and document everything in the open as we go.

How do the SoundView glasses work?

A camera on the frame reads the space ahead, an onboard model identifies what matters in the scene, and the result is spoken back through bone-conduction audio — see the "How SoundView Works" section above for the full breakdown.

Can I invest or support the project?

Yes. We're currently running an $8M SAFE note seed round — details are in the Investment section above. If you'd rather support informally, the Donors page has other ways to help.

Is the hardware or software open source?

Our builds are documented publicly on the Blog and Gallery pages, including materials lists and circuit notes, so others can learn from and build on our work.
