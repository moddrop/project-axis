# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hardware makers, student engineers, and tinkerers who build things with microcontrollers, sensors, and code. Secondarily: investors and sponsors who respect hands-on technical credibility over polished pitch decks. Both audiences arrive skeptical and leave convinced (or not) based on whether the work feels real.

The primary reader is someone who has soldered something, burned a finger, and would rather read a build log than a press release.

## Product Purpose

Project Axis is a student-led initiative (Caleb 13, Binbin 12, Raver 12) building affordable AI-powered smart glasses (SoundView) to help the 2.2 billion people living with vision impairment. The site documents the full journey: experiments, failures, funding, and progress. It functions as a maker's blog, a project portfolio, and an investor pitch simultaneously. Success means a reader finishes a page and thinks "these kids are the real deal."

## Brand Personality

Raw, Electric, Earnest.

Voice: direct, fast, no filler. Talks like a build log, not a press release. Celebrates imperfection because imperfection means it actually got built. Never performs maturity, but never apologizes for age either.

## Anti-references

- AI-generated "startup landing pages": hero metric grids, gradient text, glassmorphism, identical card grids, over-produced motion
- Apple-minimalism: too expensive-feeling, too clean, wrong register for a student maker project
- Flat/bland SaaS cream: generic, forgettable, signals nothing about what the project actually is
- Corporate pitch decks rendered as HTML: removes the humanity that is the entire point

## Design Principles

1. **Show the process, not the pitch.** Circuit photos, update logs, real failures, actual component names. Authenticity beats polish every time for this audience.
2. **Hardware warmth over tech-cold.** Color temperature should feel like a workshop: amber filament bulbs, copper PCB traces, hot solder, neon flex in a dark lab — not cold blue corporate tech.
3. **Earned roughness.** Slight grit is intentional. Mono code snippets, raw measurements, build-log timestamps. The site should feel like someone who actually built something made it.
4. **Age is not a disclaimer.** The design communicates confidence and competence. No hedging, no softening. The work speaks.
5. **Every word earns its place.** No intros that repeat titles. No filler. Makers scan fast; write for that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The project exists specifically to serve people with vision impairment — accessibility is not optional. Keyboard navigation and screen reader support are required. Color must never be the sole carrier of meaning.
